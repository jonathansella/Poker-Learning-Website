Texas Hold'em Rules

Game Structure

  • The game begins with two players posting forced bets:
    • Small Blind: Usually half the minimum bet
    • Big Blind: Usually the minimum bet
  • The dealer button (indicating the theoretical dealer) moves clockwise after each hand
  • Each player receives two private cards (hole cards)

Betting Rounds

  • Pre-flop: First betting round after receiving hole cards
    • Action starts with player left of the big blind
    • Players must at least call the big blind to stay in
  • Flop: Three community cards are dealt
    • Action starts with first active player left of button
    • Players can check if no previous bet
  • Turn: Fourth community card is dealt
    • Betting round follows same structure as flop
    • Bet sizes often double on turn in limit games
  • River: Final community card is dealt
    • Final betting round
    • Showdown if multiple players remain

Player Actions

  • Check: Pass the action when no bet to call
  • Bet: Place a wager when no previous bet exists
  • Call: Match the current bet amount
  • Raise: Increase the current bet amount
  • Fold: Discard your hand and forfeit the pot
  • All-in: Bet all remaining chips

Position and Strategy

  • Early Position (EP): First to act, play tight
  • Middle Position (MP): More flexibility in hand selection
  • Late Position (LP): Most advantageous, can play more hands
  • Button (BTN): Best position, last to act post-flop

Showdown Rules

  • Best five-card hand wins the pot
  • Can use any combination of hole cards and community cards
  • In case of identical hands, pot is split
  • Players must show cards to win pot
